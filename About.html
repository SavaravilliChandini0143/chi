KFC, originally known as Kentucky Fried Chicken, is a global fast-food restaurant chain specializing in fried chicken. Colonel Sanders began selling fried chicken from his roadside restaurant in Corbin, Kentucky, during the Great Depression. Recognizing the potential of franchising, he opened the first KFC franchise in South Salt Lake, Utah, in 1952. The brand quickly gained popularity, becoming one of the first Western fast-food chains to expand internationally, with outlets in Canada, the UK, Mexico, and Jamaica by the mid-1960
